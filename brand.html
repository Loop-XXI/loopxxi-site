Voice & Tone

✓ Do

  • Calm, direct, confident
  • Tech company first — Bitcoin is a treasury asset
  • Emphasize compounding, durability, recursion
  • Quantify: "sats earned", "products shipped"
  • AI-first operations, systematic scaling
  • Let the work speak — restraint over hype

✕ Don't

  • Lead with "Bitcoin company" in public copy
  • Use orange as a brand color
  • Blockchain/crypto buzzwords on homepage
  • Overpromise or hype-cycle language
  • Hard-tie the brand to one protocol or asset
  • Verbose explanations — cut to the essential

Brand Usage Rules

1Always write the company as LoopXXI (one word, "XXI" roman numeral) or Loop21 in legal contexts.
2The loop-arc mark must always have clear space equal to at least half its diameter on all sides.
3Do not recolor the mark. Use Obsidian on light backgrounds, Bone on dark. No tinted versions.
4The wordmark suffix "XXI" is always lighter than "Loop" — never bold or matching weight.
5No decorative elements around the logo: no drop shadows, glows, gradients, or containing boxes.
6Primary typeface is Inter. Fallback: -apple-system, BlinkMacSystemFont, sans-serif.
7Homepage copy should describe LoopXXI as a technology and software company, not a "Bitcoin company."
